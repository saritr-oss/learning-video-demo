[Slide 2] The Reality vs. The Trigger 
When does the company actually 'own' the goods? 


Physical vs. Financial: 
You received a box! It's right there, physically on your desk. But for the company's money 
records, it doesn't officially exist yet. Think of it like a sports game: you have the ball (physical 
delivery), but it doesn't officially count for the score until you *make the shot* (digital Goods 
Receipt action). Without that shot, the scorekeeper never sees it! 


The Trigger: Your Action -> Its Impact 
The big change from "a box on a desk" to "what the company officially owes or owns on paper" 
happens when you take a specific digital action: the Goods Receipt (GR). This digital entry is 
your "Trigger." Without it, the delivery is invisible to Accounting. No one knows the company 
should pay for it.

Step 1: Initiating the Receipt in Coupa 
Your First Action: Start the Digital Receipt 
We begin in Coupa, our main online system. 


Your Task: 


1. *Log in* to Coupa. 
2. *Find* your specific Purchase Order (PO) in the 'Activity' section. 
3. *Click* the Truck Icon. Think of the 'Truck Icon' as your 'Go!' button – the moment you press 
it, the digital receipt process begins! This is your key to unlocking the receiving page. 
Did you spot the 'Truck Icon' in your mind? It's your key to unlocking the next step! 


[Slide 5] Step 2: Quantity & The ‘Tolerance’ Risk 
Your Entry, Your Impact: Avoiding Phantom Inventory 
Once you click the Truck Icon, you'll go to the "Receiving Entry" screen. 


Your Task: 


1. *Enter* the Receipt Date. Make sure this matches when the goods actually arrived. 
2. *Type* the Quantity Received. If you got 5 but ordered 10, just enter 5. 
What happens if you make a mistake? 
Currently, our system has No Tolerance Set. This means if you ordered 5 laptops but 
accidentally type "6" in the quantity field, the system will currently accept it. 


Consequence: This creates "Phantom Inventory." Entering '6' when you received '5' is like 
telling your friends you have an extra pizza slice that *doesn't actually exist* for the party. It's a 
'Phantom Pizza' – on paper, but nowhere to be found! This creates a financial liability for an 
item that isn't real. 


Your Personal Safeguard (Best Practice): 
Always *double-check by physically counting* the boxes next to you against the Purchase Order 
quantity before you click submit. Your eyes are the best defense! Don't rely on the system to 
stop you. 


Imagine you're physically counting boxes. If you accidentally type the wrong number, what

Made a Mistake? Don’t Delete. Void. 
Your Correction Power: Fix Mistakes Safely 
Human error happens! You might enter the wrong quantity or receive the wrong Purchase 
Order. 


Golden Rule: Never Delete, Always Void! 
In financial systems, we never just "delete" history as if it never happened. Think of it like 
writing in a notebook: Deleting is like ripping a page out – it's gone forever, no one knows what 



happened. Voiding is like drawing a neat, clear line through the error and writing the correction 
next to it – the original mistake is still there, but you know it's fixed, and you can still see the 
whole story! 


Your Action: 


1. If you made an error, *find* the receipt in your history. 
2. *Select* "Void Receipt." 
Your Result: This action creates a "Void Consumption" document. It's like a special negative 
entry that makes the numbers balance out in the ledger, but it keeps a clear record that an 
error happened and was fixed. This preserves the trail for any questions later. 


Which action lets you retrace your steps later, like following a trail: deleting or voiding? 


[Slide 7] Why ‘Void’ Instead of ‘Delete’? 
Why is 'Voiding' the Smart Choice for an Audit? 
Why do we do this "Void Consumption" thing? Because financial systems need a perfect Audit 
Trail. Imagine you're leaving breadcrumbs on a trail (voiding) versus sweeping your tracks clean 
(deleting). If an auditor (a detective for money!) comes along, which trail helps them find their 
way back to what happened? Voiding gives them the 'breadcrumbs'! 


Here’s the difference: 


* Deleting: Erases history. It hides the mistake. If an auditor asks, "What happened here?", 
there is no record, just a blank space. 
* Voiding: Keeps a record of the error (+5 units) and the immediate correction (-5 units). It 
shows the full, honest story. 
Key Term: Void Consumption. Remember this! It’s the sign of a clear, transparent way to fix 
mistakes. 


Picture yourself as the auditor. Which record (deleted or voided) would give you the complete, 
honest story?

The 5-Minute Heartbeat 
The Digital Delay: Why You Must Wait 
After you submit your receipt (or the void) in Coupa, you might want to check SAP immediately. 
But hold on! 


Why the Wait? 
Coupa and SAP are separate systems. They don't update instantly. The 5-minute sync cycle is 
like baking a cake – you can't open the oven door every second to check if it's done! You have 
to wait for the system to 'bake' your entry before it's ready in SAP. 


Your Rule: 
* *Wait at least 5 minutes* after submitting in Coupa before checking SAP. 



Your Confirmation: 
If you check too early, the record won't be there. You are waiting for the system to generate a 
Material Document Number (a unique ID usually starting with 5000). It's like seeing the 'Done!' 
light on the oven. 


Set a mental timer! What happens if you check SAP too soon, before the 'cake' is baked? 


[Slide 9] 

Step 3: Verifying the Truth in SAP 
Your Destination: Finding the Financial Truth 
Once the 5 minutes have passed, we move to SAP to verify the "Financial Truth." 


Your Goal: 
We are looking for the 'Material Document'. This is the ultimate, official proof for Accounting 
that the liability is recognized. 


Your Navigation Steps: 


1. *Open* the Purchase Order (PO) in SAP. 
2. *Go to* the Item Details view (the bottom section of the screen). 
3. *Click* on the Purchase Order History tab. 
Think of this tab as your financial 'Bank Statement' – it's literally where the company's money 
'speaks' the truth about the delivery! It proves the transaction actually cleared.

[Slide 10] 

Decoding the SAP Record 
Your Verification Mission: Confirm the Details 
Inside the Purchase Order History tab, you'll see a grid of data. You need to verify three specific 
things to make sure everything is accurate: 


Your Tasks: 


1. *Find* the MvT (Movement Type): Look for the code 101. This is the standard SAP code for a 
Goods Receipt. Think of MvT 101 as the 'Goods Received' *stamp* – it tells you exactly what 
happened. 
2. *Find* the Mat. Doc. (Material Document): This is the unique ID number generated by SAP 
(e.g., 5000028491). The Mat. Doc. is like the delivery's *unique ID card*. 
3. *Verify* the Quantity: Check that the amount listed here matches exactly what you entered 
in Coupa.

[Slide 11] Connecting the Dots 



Your Detective Work: Find the Coupa Connection 
Your Problem: How do we know which SAP entry matches which Coupa action, especially if 
there are multiple receipts? 


Your Solution: 


1. *Click* the Material Document number (the 5000... number) to open the document details. 
2. *Look* at the 'Header Text' field. 
Your Proof (The Link): The integration automatically copies the original Coupa GR Number into 
the SAP Header Text. This is like attaching a tiny *GPS tracker* (your Coupa GR Number) to 
your SAP record. If anyone ever asks, 'Where did this SAP entry come from?', you can easily 
*click and show them the GPS tracker* in the 'Header Text'! This permanently links the two 
systems. If you are ever audited, this is your secret weapon to trace the path back to the origin.
